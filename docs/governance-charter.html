Purpose and Scope

Purpose

This charter establishes the governance framework for ensuring digital accessibility across all University of Arizona websites, applications, documents, and digital content.

Goals

  • Ensure compliance with ADA Title II and WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards
  • Create consistent accessibility practices across all units
  • Establish clear accountability and decision-making authority
  • Provide resources and support for accessibility implementation
  • Foster a culture of inclusive design

Scope

This charter applies to:

  • All university-owned websites and web applications
  • Mobile applications published by the university
  • Digital documents and multimedia
  • Third-party platforms used for university services
  • Learning management system content
  • Communications and marketing materials

Governance Structure

Organizational Model

The accessibility governance structure operates at three levels:

  1. Executive Sponsor:Senior leadership providing strategic direction and resources
  2. Accessibility Steering Committee:Cross-functional leadership setting priorities and policies
  3. Working Groups:Functional teams implementing accessibility in their areas

Executive Sponsor

  • Role:Chief Information Officer (CIO) or designee
  • Responsibilities:
    • Champions accessibility at executive level
    • Approves policies and major initiatives
    • Allocates resources
    • Reports to university leadership

Roles and Responsibilities

Digital Accessibility Director

  • Leads the accessibility program
  • Chairs the Steering Committee
  • Develops and maintains accessibility standards
  • Provides consultation and support
  • Monitors compliance and reports metrics
  • Manages accessibility testing and remediation
  • Coordinates training initiatives

Unit Accessibility Liaisons

Each college and major administrative unit designates an Accessibility Liaison who:

  • Serves as the accessibility point of contact for their unit
  • Coordinates accessibility efforts within their unit
  • Participates in the Accessibility Champions network
  • Attends quarterly liaison meetings
  • Tracks unit-level accessibility metrics
  • Escalates issues to the Digital Accessibility Director

Content Creators

  • Create accessible content (web, documents, media)
  • Complete required accessibility training
  • Use accessibility checkers before publishing
  • Respond to accessibility feedback

Developers

  • Build accessible websites and applications
  • Follow WCAG 2.1 AA standards
  • Conduct accessibility testing during development
  • Remediate accessibility defects

Managers and Supervisors

  • Ensure staff complete accessibility training
  • Include accessibility in job responsibilities
  • Allocate time for accessibility work
  • Support accessibility initiatives

Key Processes

Policy Development

  1. Working Group or member proposes policy change
  2. Digital Accessibility Director drafts policy
  3. Steering Committee reviews and provides feedback
  4. Legal and stakeholder review
  5. Executive Sponsor approval
  6. Communication and implementation

New Project Review

All new web and application projects must:

  1. Include accessibility requirements in project scope
  2. Notify Digital Accessibility Director at project initiation
  3. Include accessibility testing in QA plan
  4. Complete accessibility review before launch
  5. Document accessibility status

Procurement Review

Technology purchases must:

  1. Include accessibility requirements in RFP/RFQ
  2. Request VPAT/ACR from vendors
  3. Have VPAT reviewed by Digital Accessibility team
  4. Include accessibility terms in contracts
  5. Document any exceptions with remediation plan

See Procurement Process

Complaint Handling

  1. Complaint received via accessibility feedback form
  2. Acknowledged within 2 business days
  3. Assigned to appropriate owner
  4. Resolution or accommodation within timeframe based on severity
  5. Documentation in tracking system
  6. Follow-up with complainant

Accountability and Reporting

Metrics and KPIs

The accessibility program tracks and reports on:

  • Percentage of websites meeting WCAG 2.1 AA
  • Number and severity of accessibility issues
  • Time to resolve accessibility complaints
  • Training completion rates
  • Vendor accessibility compliance
  • Document remediation volume

Charter Review

Review Cycle

This charter will be reviewed and updated:

  • Annually by the Steering Committee
  • When significant regulatory changes occur
  • When organizational structure changes
  • Upon request of Executive Sponsor

Amendment Process

  1. Proposed changes submitted to Steering Committee
  2. Committee review and discussion
  3. Stakeholder input as needed
  4. Executive Sponsor approval for significant changes
  5. Communication of updates
