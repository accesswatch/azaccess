General

What is digital accessibility?

Digital accessibility means designing websites, applications, documents, and content so that everyone can use them—including people with visual, hearing, motor, or cognitive disabilities.

This includes making sure content works with screen readers, can be navigated by keyboard, has captions for videos, and uses clear language.

Learn more in Accessibility 101

Why does accessibility matter?

Equity:About 1 in 4 adults has a disability. Everyone deserves equal access to education and information.

Legal:Title II of the ADA requires public universities to provide accessible digital experiences.

Better design:Accessible design benefits everyone—captions help non-native speakers, keyboard navigation helps power users, clear content helps everyone under stress.

What accessibility standard does Arizona follow?

Arizona aligns toWCAG 2.2 Level AA—the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines published by the W3C (World Wide Web Consortium).

This is the most widely adopted international standard and is referenced by ADA Title II regulations.

Students

How do I get accommodations?

Register with theDisability Resource Center (DRC). You'll submit documentation, meet with an Access Consultant, and receive accommodation letters to share with your instructors.

The process takes about 15 minutes online plus a meeting. Start early in the semester!

How do I request captions for a video?

Most Panopto videos have auto-generated captions. If they're inaccurate or missing:

  1. Ask your instructor to request caption edits
  2. Or contactUCATT Multimediadirectly for urgent needs

For live events, request CART or ASL interpreters through theDRC ASL/CART form.

How do I report an accessibility barrier?

Use theAccess Reportform. Include:

  • The URL or location of the problem
  • What you were trying to do
  • What went wrong
  • Screenshots if possible

You'll receive a response within 2 business days. Reporting helps Arizona fix issues for everyone.

Faculty

How do I respond to an accommodation letter?

Reply within2 business days:

  1. Read the letter carefully—note specific accommodations
  2. Contact the student to confirm logistics
  3. Document agreements in Brightspace or email
  4. Reach out to the DRC if you have questions

Never deny accommodations or require students to justify their needs beyond the letter.

How do I make my Brightspace course accessible?

Start with ourBrightspace Accessibility Checklist. Key steps:

  • Use heading styles for structure
  • Add alt text to images
  • Check color contrast
  • Caption videos in Panopto
  • Run the built-in accessibility checker

UCATT offers consultations and can review your course shell.

How do I caption my lecture videos?

Upload to Panopto, which generates automatic captions. Then:

  1. Review and edit the captions for accuracy
  2. Focus on names, technical terms, and numbers
  3. For high-stakes content, request professional captions from UCATT

Aim for 99% accuracy on instructional content.See the full captioning guide

Documents

How do I check if my Word/PowerPoint is accessible?

Use the built-in Accessibility Checker:

  1. Go toReview Check Accessibility
  2. Fix all errors (red) and review warnings (yellow)
  3. Pay special attention to missing alt text and heading structure

Microsoft's guide to the checker

How do I make a PDF accessible?

Best approach:Create an accessible source document (Word) first, then export to PDF.

For existing PDFs:

  1. Open in Adobe Acrobat Pro
  2. Run Accessibility Check (Tools Accessibility)
  3. Add tags, set reading order, add alt text

For complex remediation,request helpfrom the Digital Accessibility team.

See the full PDF remediation guide

How do I write good alt text?

Alt text should convey thepurposeof the image in context. Ask: "Why is this image here?"

  • Be concise (usually 1-2 sentences)
  • Don't start with "Image of..." or "Picture of..."
  • For decorative images, use empty alt text (alt="")
  • For charts/graphs, describe the key takeaway

Web & Apps

How do I test if my website is accessible?

Combine automated and manual testing:

Automated (catches ~30-40% of issues):

  • WAVE browser extension
  • Accessibility Insights
  • axe DevTools

Manual (essential):

  • Keyboard-only navigation
  • Screen reader testing (NVDA, VoiceOver)
  • Zoom to 200% and check layout
What is keyboard accessibility?

Users must be able to use your site with keyboard alone (no mouse). Test by:

  1. Tabthrough the page—can you reach everything?
  2. Is focus always visible (outline or highlight)?
  3. Can youEnterto activate buttons and links?
  4. Can youEscapeto close dialogs?

Procurement

What is a VPAT and how do I read it?

AVPAT(Voluntary Product Accessibility Template) is a document vendors create to describe how their product meets accessibility standards.

Look for:

  • "Supports"– meets the criteria
  • "Partially Supports"– some gaps (read the remarks)
  • "Does Not Support"– fails the criteria

VPATs vary widely in quality. Request a demo with assistive technology for critical purchases.

VPAT review guide

How do I know if software I want to buy is accessible?

Before purchasing:

  1. Ask the vendor for a VPAT (current version, ideally WCAG 2.2)
  2. Request their accessibility roadmap
  3. Ask for a demo using screen reader or keyboard-only
  4. Involve the Digital Accessibility team for major purchases

Legal & Compliance

What is Title II and when do we need to comply?

Title II of the ADArequires state and local governments (including public universities) to make their programs, services, and activities accessible—including digital ones.

The DOJ published updated web accessibility regulations in 2024 with specific deadlines for compliance. Large public entities like Arizona must meet WCAG 2.1 AA by April 2026.

ADA.gov web accessibility guidance

Can we get an exception for something that can't be made accessible?

Exceptions are rare and require formal documentation. Two legal defenses exist:

  • Fundamental alteration:Making it accessible would fundamentally change the nature of the program
  • Undue burden:The cost/effort would be excessive relative to resources

Both require documented alternative access plans and approval from Accessibility PMO, Legal, and executive sponsors. Contactaccessibility@arizona.eduto discuss.
