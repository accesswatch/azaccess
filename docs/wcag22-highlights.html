WCAG 2.2 Highlights

Updated: 2025-11-20 · Next review: 2026-02-20

What is WCAG 2.2?

WCAG 2.2 is the latest version of the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines. Arizona is aligning to level AA across websites, apps, documents, and LMS content as part of our Title II program.

This page summarizes the areas that change design and engineering decisions most for campus teams.

New or updated success criteria that matter most

ID Name What it means Typical UA impact
2.4.11 Focus Not Obscured (Minimum) Keyboard focus indicators cannot be hidden behind sticky headers, chat widgets, or banners. Quickstart sites with fixed headers; apps with custom focus outlines; third-party widgets.
2.4.12 Focus Not Obscured (Enhanced) At AAA, focus indicators must be fully visible; we treat this as a strong design goal. Design systems and shared components; modal dialogs; slide-over panels.
2.4.13 Focus Appearance Focus outlines must be clearly visible, with sufficient contrast and minimum size. Buttons, links, and custom components in Arizona Digital and Quickstart.
2.5.7 Dragging Movements Interfaces that require dragging (sliders, drag-and-drop) must offer a simple alternative. Course tools, lab simulations, and custom research dashboards.
2.5.8 Target Size (Minimum) Tap/click targets should be at least 24 by 24 CSS pixels. Navigation menus, icon buttons, mobile app controls, data grid actions.

What this means for different roles

Designers

Use design tokens that bake in contrast, minimum target sizes, and focus styles. Avoid layouts where sticky UI overlaps focus rings.

Developers

Use native HTML controls where possible, respect the UA focus outline, and test with keyboard-only users for every feature.

Content creators

Keep headings, links, and buttons descriptive. Avoid instructions that rely on color or position alone.
