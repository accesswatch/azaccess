Accessibility Requirements

What accessibility standard does UA require?

The University of Arizona requires all ICT products and services to conform toWCAG 2.2 Level AA. This aligns with:

  • ADA Title II requirements for state/local government
  • Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act
  • Arizona state IT accessibility policy

Do these requirements apply to all products?

Yes, accessibility requirements apply to all ICT procurements, including:

  • Web applications and SaaS platforms
  • Desktop and mobile applications
  • Learning management systems and courseware
  • Document management systems
  • Communication and collaboration tools
  • Hardware with embedded software
  • Custom development projects

What if our product doesn't meet all requirements?

Few products are 100% accessible. We evaluate products based on:

  • Current state:How many WCAG criteria are supported?
  • Impact:Do issues block essential functionality?
  • Workarounds:Are there accessible alternatives?
  • Roadmap:What's your plan to remediate issues?
  • Commitment:Is accessibility part of your development process?

We work with vendors on remediation plans when products don't fully meet requirements but show commitment to accessibility.

Are there exceptions to these requirements?

Limited exceptions may be granted when:

  • No accessible alternative exists in the market
  • The product serves a very limited audience
  • Accessibility modifications would fundamentally alter the product
  • A compliant version is in active development with timeline

All exceptions require documentation and an Equally Effective Alternate Access Plan (EEAAP).

VPAT & Documentation

What is a VPAT and why do you need one?

AVPAT(Voluntary Product Accessibility Template) is a standardized document that reports how your product conforms to accessibility standards. When completed, it becomes anACR(Accessibility Conformance Report).

We require VPATs to:

  • Evaluate accessibility before purchase
  • Compare products objectively
  • Identify needed accommodations
  • Track vendor commitment to accessibility

What VPAT version should we use?

Please useVPAT 2.5(or 2.4 minimum), which covers:

  • WCAG 2.2 (or 2.1) success criteria
  • Revised Section 508 standards
  • EN 301 549 (European standard)

How detailed should our VPAT be?

A quality VPAT includes:

  • Specific explanationsfor all "Partially Supports" and "Does Not Support" ratings
  • Workaroundsfor known issues
  • Remediation timelinesfor planned fixes
  • Testing methodologydescription
  • Product version and dateclearly stated

Avoid vague statements like "some issues may exist." Be specific about what works and what doesn't.

Who should complete our VPAT?

VPATs can be completed by:

  • Internal accessibility teams- If you have trained accessibility specialists
  • Third-party auditors- Provides independent validation (preferred for high-stakes purchases)
  • Combined approach- Internal testing validated by external review

We give more weight to third-party validated VPATs and those with detailed testing methodology.

How often should we update our VPAT?

Update your VPAT:

  • With eachmajor product release
  • Whenaccessibility improvementsare released
  • At leastannually, even without major changes
  • WhenWCAG or Section 508 standardsare updated

VPATs older than 18 months are considered outdated.

Compliance & Testing

How do we test our product for accessibility?

Comprehensive accessibility testing includes:

  1. Automated testing:Tools like axe, WAVE, or Lighthouse
  2. Manual testing:Keyboard navigation, screen reader testing
  3. User testing:Testing with people with disabilities

Automated tools catch 30-40% of issues. Manual testing is essential for complete coverage.

What screen readers should we test with?

At minimum, test with:

  • NVDA + Chrome/Firefox(Windows) - Free
  • VoiceOver + Safari(macOS/iOS) - Built-in
  • JAWS + Chrome(Windows) - If enterprise users are a primary audience

For mobile apps, also test with TalkBack (Android).

What does "WCAG Level AA" actually require?

WCAG 2.2 Level AA includes 57 success criteria organized under four principles:

  • Perceivable:Alt text, captions, color contrast, text resize
  • Operable:Keyboard access, timing, navigation, input modalities
  • Understandable:Readable text, predictable behavior, error handling
  • Robust:Compatible with assistive technologies

Contracts & RFPs

Will accessibility be in our contract?

Yes. UA contracts typically include accessibility language requiring:

  • Conformance to WCAG 2.2 Level AA
  • Remediation of reported issues within specified timeframes
  • Updated VPATs with major releases
  • Notification of accessibility regressions
  • Right to audit or request third-party testing

How is accessibility weighted in RFP scoring?

Accessibility typically accounts for10-20%of RFP scoring, depending on the product. Evaluation considers:

  • VPAT quality and completeness
  • Current conformance level
  • Remediation roadmap
  • Vendor accessibility commitment and resources
  • Third-party validation

What if we're a small company without accessibility resources?

We understand resource constraints. We recommend:

  • Use free tools like WAVE and axe to start testing
  • Follow accessibility basics (semantic HTML, keyboard access, color contrast)
  • Be honest about current limitations in your VPAT
  • Provide a realistic remediation timeline
  • Consider partnering with an accessibility consultant for initial audit

Commitment to improvement matters as much as current state.

Remediation & Support

What if UA reports accessibility issues after purchase?

Our contracts specify remediation requirements:

  • Critical issues(blocking access): 30-day remediation
  • Major issues(significant barriers): 90-day remediation
  • Minor issues(limited impact): Next major release

We work collaboratively with vendors on reasonable timelines when issues are complex.

How should we handle accessibility support requests?

Best practices for accessibility support:

  • Train support staff on accessibility basics
  • Provide a dedicated accessibility contact or channel
  • Respond to accessibility issues within 48 hours
  • Track accessibility issues separately in your bug system
  • Communicate fixes and workarounds to reporting parties

Who at UA can we contact about accessibility requirements?

Contact the Digital Accessibility Office:

  • VPAT reviews:Submit with procurement requests
  • Technical questions:Available during procurement process

For procurement-specific questions, contact your UA procurement representative.
