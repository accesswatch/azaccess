Impactful principles & tools

Procurement, events, and departmental sites stay compliant when you weave WCAG 2.2 AA into every workflow. Reference the WCAG 2.2 highlights plus these toolkits.

Procurement checkpoints
  • Require VPATs that cite WCAG 2.2 AA success criteria (focus appearance, target size, accessible authentication).
  • Ask vendors to demo Accessibility Insights or axe scans and provide DubBot-compatible URLs for monitoring.

Document interim accommodations when criteria fail.

Departmental sites & Quickstart pages
  • Run DubBot weekly and WAVE before publishing campaign pages.
  • Use Accessibility Insights FastPass for high-traffic forms or micro-sites.
  • Log results in your unit’s remediation tracker so leadership sees progress.
  • Review link text with the Meaningful Links guide so navigation and CTAs stay descriptive.
Documents, PDFs, and email attachments

Before distributing HR forms, finance packets, or newsletters:

  • Run the Microsoft Accessibility Checker in Word/Excel/PowerPoint.
  • Use Adobe PDF remediation to confirm reading order, tags, and form labels.
  • Apply WebAIM alt text principles for charts and infographics.

Guidance

Procurement workflow
  1. Follow the Accessible Procurement Steps.
  2. Attach VPATs and demo recordings showing AT compatibility (JAWS, NVDA, ZoomText).
  3. Document remediation commitments and interim accommodations before purchasing.
Event planning

Use the Inclusive Events checklist to secure ASL/CART, accessible seating, and clear signage. Provide materials in accessible formats 48 hours prior.

Departmental sites

Leverage Quickstart’s accessible components, run Lighthouse/axe scans quarterly, and request consultations for complex widgets or vendor embeds.

  • Spot-check pages with WebAIM WAVE to catch heading, contrast, and alt-text issues quickly.
  • Use Deque’s axe DevTools guidance for detailed issue paths and remediation tips.

Assistive technologies to consider

Administrative workflows (HR, finance, student services) must support community members using:

  • JAWS and NVDA for Windows-based enterprise forms.
  • VoiceOver and TalkBack for mobile-responsive sites and kiosk experiences.
  • Fusion and ZoomText for magnification in ticketing, CRM, and financial systems.

Consult the Assistive Technology Coverage doc before deploying or renewing tools.
